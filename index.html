About Me

I am a spatial thinker, educator, and builder who moves comfortably between research, code, and the classroom. My path has been anything but linear — I began as a software engineer in Mumbai, found my way to the desert Southwest for graduate school, fell in love with watersheds and maps, and never really left.

I hold a Ph.D. and M.S. in Natural Resources (Watershed Management & Ecohydrology) from the University of Arizona, and a B.Tech in Information Technology from Veermata Jijabai Technological Institute in Mumbai, India. That combination of computer science roots and environmental science training shapes everything I do — whether I'm writing Python to analyze spatial data, modeling how green infrastructure affects urban flooding, or helping students publish their first web map.

My research focuses on urban hydrology, green infrastructure, and groundwater recharge in arid and semi-arid regions. I am a co-investigator on the Arizona Tri-University Recharge and Reliability Project, and Assistant Director of Mapping Racist Covenants — a community GIS project documenting racially restrictive housing covenants in Tucson that I am particularly proud of.

Outside of work, I am a fish keeper, a salsa dancer, a volunteer, and someone who believes that showing up for your community is not optional. Those experiences — leading the Desert Aquarist Society, volunteering at the Arizona-Sonora Desert Museum, serving on the board of Desert Spring Children's Center — are as much a part of who I am as anything on my CV.

Projects

A selection of research, GIS, and web projects spanning hydrological modeling, spatial analysis, community mapping, and web development.

Mapping Racist Covenants

A public GIS project documenting racially restrictive housing covenants in Tucson, AZ. Built with J. Jurjevich, C. Kollen, and student collaborators. Funded by a $60,000 grant.

Southern AZ Golden Retriever Rescue

Ongoing Squarespace website maintenance for this nonprofit organization, managing content updates, donation systems, adoption listings, and event information.

WebGIS with Leaflet

Client-side web mapping application using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and the Leaflet library. Features custom-styled GeoJSON layers, popups, and layer controls. Deployed via GitHub Pages.

PostGIS Spatial Database

PostGIS-enabled PostgreSQL database using Docker and GitHub Codespaces. Spatial queries combining NYC neighborhoods, streets, census blocks, and subway data.

Desert Aquarist Society

Wix website designed and built from scratch for the Desert Aquarist Society (est. 1974), a Tucson-based aquarium hobbyist club. Ongoing maintenance and content updates as club President.

OSM & PostGIS Spatial Analysis

End-to-end spatial workflow using OpenStreetMap data, PostGIS, and Python. Downloads OSM shapefiles from Geofabrik, loads them into PostGIS, and runs spatial SQL queries across Arizona study areas.
